Animal Profiles

Brian

Giant African Land Snail
Scientific Name: Achatina fulica
Class: Gastropod
Favourite Food: Cucumber

Fun Fact: Giant African Land Snails, like all snails, are hermaphrodites. This means that they have both female and male sex organs, so it doesn’t matter which two snails you put together they will be able to produce eggs

Animal Profiles

Spear

Lesser Tenrec
Scientific Name: Echinops telfairi
Class: Mammal
Favourite Food: Meal worms

Fun Fact: There are nearly 30 different species of Tenrec, some of which have adapted to life in the trees. Other species have adapted to life on the ground, whilst others even live in water

Animal Profiles

Mocha and Latte

Guinea Pigs
Scientific Name: Cavia porcellus
Class: Mammals
Favourite Food: Romaine lettuce and hay

Fun Fact: Unlike humans Guinea Pigs cannot sweat, this means that they are at risk of heat stroke if they are left out in the sun or become too hot. They do love to sit in the shade whenever possible

Animal Profiles

Woody & Allen

Indian Stick Insects
Scientific Name: Carausius morosus
Class: Insects
Favourite Food: Privet

Fun Fact: Once a nymph (immature insect) has moulted (shed its skin) it will usually eat its shrivelled exoskeleton. One reason they do this is to recycle the protein instead of letting it go to waste

Animal Profiles

Alto

Barn Owl
Scientific Name: Tyto alba alba
Class: Bird
Favourite Food: Small rats

Fun Fact: Barn owls have lop sided ears, one ear being lower than the other. This is so that they can pinpoint their prey exactly even if they cannot see it because of long grass

Animal Profiles

Tango

Bearded Dragon
Scientific Name: Pogona vitticeps
Class: Reptile
Favourite Food: Locusts

Fun Fact: Bearded dragons have a variety of behaviours and displays; one of the popular ones is puffing their throat out. If they puff it out and it goes black this means they are angry, but if it doesn’t change colour they are most likely just yawning or stretching

Popcorn

Corn Snake
Scientific Name: Pantherophis guttatus
Class: Reptile
Favourite Food: Mice

Fun Fact: These snakes got their name 2 reasons, 1) they are often found in cornfields hunting for rodents, and 2) the scales on their belly looks like the kernels of Indian corn

Pinstripe Ball Python

Class: Reptile
Favourite Food: Mice

Class: Diplopod
Favourite Food: Cucumber
