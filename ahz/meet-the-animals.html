Brian - Giant African Land Snail

Scientific Name: Achatina fulica

Class: Gastropod

Favourite Food: Cucumber

Fun Fact: Giant African Land Snails, like all snails, are hermaphrodites. This means that they have both female and male sex organs, so it doesn't matter which two snails you put together they will be able to produce eggs.

Spear - Lesser Tenrec

Scientific Name: Echinops telfairi

Class: Mammal

Favourite Food: Meal Worms

Fun Fact: Fun Fact: There are nearly 30 different species of Tenrec, some of which have adapted to life in the trees. Other species have adapted to life on the ground, whilst others even live in water.

Mocha and Latte - Guinea Pigs

Scientific Name: Cavia porcellus

Class: Mammals

Favourite Food: Romaine lettuce and hay

Fun Fact: Unlike humans Guinea Pigs cannot sweat, this means that they are at risk of heat stroke if they are left out in the sun or become too hot. They do love to sit in the shade whenever possible.

Pumpkin and Winnie - Skinny Pig and Werewolf Skinny Pig

Scientific Name: Cavia porcellus

Class: Mammals

Favourite Food: Corn on the cob and hay

Fun Fact: These hairless Guinea Pigs are the result of a laboratory experiment and were first created during 1978 in Canada.

Woody and Allen - Indian Stick Insects

Scientific Name: Carausius morosus

Class: Insects

Favourite Food: Privet

Fun Fact: Once a nymph (immature insect) has moulted (shed its skin) it will usually eat its shrivelled exoskeleton. One reason they do this is to recycle the protein instead of letting it go to waste.

Alto - Barn Owl

Scientific Name: Tyto alba alba

Class: Bird

Favourite Food: Small rats

Fun Fact: Barn owls have lop sided ears, one ear being lower than the other. This is so that they can pinpoint their prey exactly even if they cannot see it because of long grass.

Tango - Bearded Dragon

Scientific Name: Pogona vitticeps

Class: Reptile

Favourite Food: Locusts

Fun Fact: Bearded dragons have a variety of behaviours and displays; one of the popular ones is puffing their throat out. If they puff it out and it goes black this means they are angry, but if it doesn't change colour they are most likely just yawning or stretching.

Dennis - Duprasi

Scientific Name: Pachyuromys dupras

Class: Mammal

Favourite Food: Kale and Mealworms

Fun Fact: They like to sleep on their backs with their tiny feet pointing upwards.

Popcorn - Corn Snake

Scientific Name: Pantherophis guttatus

Class: Reptile

Favourite Food: Mice

Fun Fact: These snakes got their name for two reasons, 1) they are often found in cornfields hunting for rodents, and 2) the scales on their belly looks like the kernels of Indian corn.

Pretzel - Pinstripe Ball Python

Scientific Name: Python regius

Class: Reptile

Favourite Food: Mice

Fun Fact: Ball Pythons are one of the most popular pet snake, this is because they are much smaller than other python species only growing to an average of 3 - 5 feet long. Also, with good socialisation, they can be very docile snakes.

Laura - Butter Ball Python

Scientific Name: Python regius

Class: Reptile

Favourite Food: Mice

Fun Fact: Ball Pythons get their name because when they feel threatened they tend to curl up into a ball. Some people call them Royal Pythons because ancient royalty wore them as jewelry.

Peeka - Leopard Gecko

Scientific Name: Eublepharis macularius

Class: Reptile

Favourite Food: Crickets

Fun Fact: They have a long tongue that they use to clean their eyes.

Ant and Dec - Madagascar Hissing Cockroaches

Scientific Name: Gromphadorhina portentosa

Class: Insects

Favourite Food: Apple

Fun Fact: Most animals that hiss make the noise by rubbing their body parts together. These Cockroaches however make their hissing noise by exhaling air quickly through their breathing holes.

PD and Socks - African Giant Train Millipedes

Scientific Name: Archispirostreptus gigas

Class: Diplopod

Favourite Food: Cucumber

Fun Fact: To tell the difference between male and females you have to look at the legs on the 7th body segment. Males have specialized legs with grasping claws called gonopods that are often tucked up under the body.

Lily - Horsefield Tortoise

Scientific Name: Testudo horsfieldii

Class: Reptile

Favourite Food: Dandelion leaves

Fun Fact: The shell of the tortoise is made up of numerous small bones that are covered by separate plates of keratin called scutes. As a tortoise grows, extra layers of keratin are added underneath the existing layer causing "growth rings".

Ocho - Chilean Rose Tarantula

Scientific Name: Grammostola rosea

Class: Arachnida

Favourite Food: Locusts

Fun Fact: Tarantulas rely on their sensory hairs for hunting; the thick, hairy covering of the body (about 1 million per spider) is extremely sensitive to vibration helping them find prey.

Richard and Parker - Giant Tiger Land Snails

Scientific Name: Achatina achatina

Class: Gastropod

Favourite Food: Fish Food Flakes

Fun Fact: Tiger snails are the largest land snail in the world with the average shell growing to 18cm (shells 30cm long have been recorded).
