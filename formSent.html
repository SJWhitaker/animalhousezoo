At Animal House Zoo the safety and well-being of our animals is of paramount importance to us. We therefore strickly adhere to the 5 freedoms:

  • FREEDOM FROM HUNGER AND THIRST
    All our animals have ready access to fresh water and a species-specific diet in order to maintain health and vigour.

  • FREEDOM FROM DISCOMFORT
    All our animals have an appropriate environment that includes shelter and a comfortable resting area.

  • FREEDOM FROM PAIN, INJURY OR DISEASE
    All our animals are seen everyday so a rapid diagnosis and treatment can be given if they become ill. They are also housed, fed and cared for correctly in order to prevent any pain, injury or disease.

  • FREEDOM TO EXPRESS NORMAL BEHAVIOUR
    All our animals have more than the minimum recommended space, proper facilities, appropriate enrichment and company of animals of its own kind (if they are not a solitary species).

  • FREEDOM FROM FEAR AND DISTRESS
    All our animals are kept in conditions that keep them comfortable and stress free. On encounters, if any of our animals are in a situation where they could become stressed they will immediately be returned to their travel carriers to prevent discomfort.
